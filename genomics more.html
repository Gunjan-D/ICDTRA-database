GENOMICS

Genomics is a multidisciplinary field within biology that focuses on various aspects of genomes, including their structure, function, evolution, mapping, and manipulation. A genome refers to the complete set of an organism's DNA, encompassing both its genes and the hierarchical, three-dimensional arrangement of the genetic material. In contrast to genetics, which primarily investigates individual genes and their inheritance patterns, genomics seeks to comprehensively characterize and quantify all the genes of an organism, exploring their interconnections and impact on the organism as a whole.
