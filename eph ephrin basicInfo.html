• Ephrins are also known as ephrin ligands or proteins that interact with Eph family receptors.
  • They are a group of proteins that act as the Eph receptor's ligands. The largest known subfamily of receptor protein-tyrosine kinases (RTKs) is made up of eph receptors.
  • Since both ephrin ligands and eph receptors are membrane-bound proteins, only direct cell-cell contact can result in the binding and activation of eph/eph intracellular signalling pathways.
  • During embryonic development, eph/ephrin signalling controls a number of biological processes, such as cell migration, segmentation, axon growth cone guidance, and the formation of tissue boundaries.
