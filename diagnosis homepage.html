Diagnostics tools

The Diagnostic tools are developed to assist general practitioners in diagnosing cancer,
although the impact remains uncertain on expediting diagnosis and their effect on patients
quality of life and survival. The aim of ICDTRA is to assess the validation, clinical effectiveness,
cost-effectiveness, availability, and utilization of cancer diagnostic tools in primary care.

CANCER CENTERS

The Government of India and the relevant state governments jointly control and fund the
cancer treatment hospitals and research institutes operating in India. ICDTRA provides details
and direct links to all conceivable cancer treatment facilities and cancer research facilities
in India. There are currently 25 such centres spread across all of India's states and union
territories.

NEW MARKERS
FOR DIAGNOSIS
When used properly, qualified biomarkers have the potential to offer useful data that could help regulatory
decisions regarding drug development become less uncertain. A biomarker has undergone a formal
regulatory process to ensure that we can rely on it to have a specific interpretation when it is
qualified. Application in the designated context of use (COU) for medical product development and
regulatory review. A biomarker is a defined characteristic that is measured as an indicator of typical
biological processes, pathogenic processes, or reactions to an exposure or intervention, including
therapeutic interventions, according to the Biomarkers, EndpointS, and other Tools (BEST) glossary.
MICROARRAY DATABASE
Microarray gene expression data are stored in a microarray database. The main functions of a microarray
database are data storage, index management, and data distribution (either directly or via user downloads) to other applications for analysis and interpretation. Microarray databases can be divided into two categories first is peer-reviewed, public repository that follows standards set by academia or business and is intended for use by a wide range of analysis applications and groups. The Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO) from NCBI or ArrayExpress from EBI are two excellent examples of this. A specialised repository, whether it is for profit or not, that is primarily linked to the name of a specific entity (lab, company, university, consortium, group), application suite, subject, or analytical technique.
HUMAN SPECIMEN FOR RESEARCH
Significant scientific and medical breakthroughs have resulted from the use of human specimens in research.
The identification of specimens and the security of participants in human specimen research have, however, come under fresh scrutiny as a result of the advancement of advanced whole genome and informatics technologies as well as the growth in specimen and data sharing.Human samples and biorepositories will continue to be essential for the development of individualised medicine and targeted treatments, as well as other scientific and medical endeavours. However, given the existing regulatory, policy, and scientific environment, its broad usage poses a number of difficulties.
